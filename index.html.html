Some of you are going to love this. Some of you are going to be furious. That's fantasy baseball. May your IL stay empty.

Team-by-Team Scouting Reports

1
Brett Favre's Pitchin Club 25
A-Rank #1 — Clear Favorite
Strengths
The deepest pitching staff in the draft — full stop. Fried, Strider (TJ rebound), Glasnow, Sheehan, Chandler, McClanahan, and Mick Abel give this team more high-ceiling SP point options than anyone else in the league. The offense is anchored by Witt Jr., Rutschman, Kurtz, and Robert Jr. with serious power upside. Sal Stewart (.355/.487/.613 this spring) is the best late-round value in the entire draft.
Concerns
Jordan Lawlar is the highest-variance player at any position — elite point producer or total bust with no middle ground. Gerrit Cole's IL status needs to be resolved before Week 1 lineups lock.
Wild Card
Luis Robert Jr. If healthy for 140+ games, the offensive point totals jump dramatically. If not, the rotation depth still carries this team to a top-3 weekly scoring finish.
Verdict
Clear favorite. The combination of pitching depth and a balanced, high-upside offense produces the most consistent weekly point totals in the league. Every other team needs this one to run into bad injury luck.
2
Every Pitchers Name I Use They
B+Rank #2 — Highest Offensive Point Ceiling
Strengths
The best pure offense in the entire draft — Soto and Harper as a 1-2 punch is elite, backed by Austin Riley, Corey Seager, and Logan O'Hoppe. That lineup generates weekly offensive points at a rate most rosters simply cannot match. Mason Miller is the second-best scoring closer in this league, and Bryan Abreu is handling closing duties while Hader recovers.
Concerns
Grayson Rodriguez is on the IL to open the season — hasn't pitched in MLB since July 2024. Jared Jones is on IL-60. The rotation is very top-heavy and weekly pitching point totals could get inconsistent if Nola and Schlittler underdeliver.
Wild Card
Grayson Rodriguez's return. If healthy by June, the pitching matches the offense and this becomes the league's most complete weekly scorer. If not, they'll outscore opponents at the plate and hope the rotation holds.
Verdict
The highest offensive point ceiling in the league. Rotation depth is the only thing separating them from the top spot. Opponents will need elite pitching weeks to survive the offensive onslaught.
3
THE Bald Brethren
B+Rank #3 — Most Balanced Weekly Scorer
Strengths
The most balanced roster in the draft from a weekly points standpoint. Skubal is a top-3 SP scoring machine with elite ratios. Cade Smith is the best high-leverage closer in the league — save and strikeout points add up fast with a 40-save ceiling. Devin Williams adds elite reliever scoring. Henderson, Suarez, and McLain provide three-position offensive depth.
Concerns
The outfield (Marsee, Caglianone, Torkelson) is the thinnest point-producing OF group in the league. If those three don't contribute offensively, the roster leans entirely on pitching to win matchups. Eury Perez is a high-upside scoring arm with real injury history.
Wild Card
Eury Perez. A full healthy season makes him a top-15 SP point scorer. An injury puts real pressure on an outfield that can't absorb it.
Verdict
True title contender. Skubal/Cade Smith alone provides a weekly scoring floor most rosters can't match. Ranked #3 over Ian's Inspiring Team on the strength of superior closer scoring output.
4
Ian's Inspiring Team
B+Rank #4 — Highest Floor in the League
Strengths
The highest weekly scoring floor in the league — this team never has a blowout loss. Jose Ramirez is an elite five-tool point generator. Yordan Alvarez is a top-5 power bat. Gallen, Peralta, and Eovaldi form a top-5 rotation scoring trio. Edwin Diaz adds high-leverage closer points every week.
Concerns
Age is a real concern — Springer (35+), Semien (35+), and Gausman (35+) all carry decline risk. Any significant drop-off from even one of them shaves meaningful points off the weekly total. Jeremy Pena starts with a fractured finger.
Wild Card
If the veterans age gracefully and Pena returns quickly, this is one of the most consistent weekly scorers in the league. If Springer or Semien slip, the offensive point floor drops noticeably.
Verdict
The safest pick to win the most H2H matchups in a given season. An elite floor means this team rarely gets blown out. Ranked #4 over Chain of Custody because they actually have a closer generating save points.
5
Chain of Custody
BRank #5 — Best Rotation, Weak Closer Scoring
Strengths
The best rotation top-to-bottom in the draft. Ohtani as a two-way point engine, plus Valdez and Cease, gives them three SP1-level scorers at the top. Chase Burns adds high-ceiling scoring depth. Tatis Jr. is a five-tool offensive point machine who can single-handedly swing a weekly matchup.
Fatal Flaw
The worst closer scoring situation in the league. Detmers is not a closer. Estrada has no saves path. Romero is buried in a murky St. Louis committee. In a points format, save bonuses accumulate into a significant weekly advantage — and this roster is giving that up every single week, all season long.
Wild Card
Chase Burns converting premium stuff into high-volume innings and strikeout points. With Greene's injury opening the Reds rotation, Burns may log more starts than expected.
Verdict
A magnificent rotation handcuffed by a bullpen that doesn't score save points. Chain of Custody and Kent Murphy's Dingers are the two teams most likely to be hunting for closer upgrades at the trade deadline.
6
Thievin' Ricans
BRank #6 — Most Underrated Weekly Scorer
Strengths
The most underrated team in the draft. Suarez and Castillo form a quietly elite weekly SP scoring duo — both generate consistent quality-start and strikeout points. Vladdy Jr. is a top-10 first base point scorer. Corbin Carroll offers speed-and-power offensive upside. Byron Buxton, when healthy, is a top-5 OF weekly scorer.
Wild Card
Justin Steele. Medically cleared from UCL revision, targeting May/June return. His return adds a legitimate SP2-level point producer and gives Thievin' Ricans a four-deep pitching scoring rotation — that's playoff-caliber in the second half.
Concerns
Closer scoring is inconsistent — Estevez (velocity concerns), Robert Garcia (unproven), Robert Suarez (not in a saves role). No elite high-leverage save scorer. Carroll's hamstring history is worth monitoring all season.
Verdict
If Steele returns in May and Buxton stays healthy, Thievin' Ricans quietly competes for a top-3 weekly scoring finish in the second half. Most opponents will underestimate this team — and regret it.
7
Kent Murphy's Dingers
BRank #7 — Elite SP Scoring, No Closer Points
Strengths
Garrett Crochet is one of the top strikeout point scorers in baseball — a legitimate Cy Young candidate. George Kirby may be the most underrated pitcher in the game (6.87 career K/BB ratio = massive quality-start points). Bibee, Pivetta, and Pepiot give a five-deep scoring rotation. When Greene (July) and Priester (late April) return, potentially 7 SP-caliber point producers.
Fatal Flaw
Vesia, Brash, and Vest are three setup men with no saves path whatsoever. In an H2H points format, save bonuses are weekly scoring that this roster simply doesn't access. The offense also has a liability at SS: Geraldo Perdomo is one of the lowest point-producing shortstops in this draft.
Wild Card
Jo Adell. The eternal boom-or-bust prospect. If it clicks in 2026 — 25 HR, 15 SB — he becomes a legitimate weekly offensive scorer. If not, the outfield is a consistent drag on the point total.
Verdict
The most lopsided roster in the draft — elite pitching point production, zero closer scoring. Will beat teams on SP points and lose on the save column every week. Ranked #7 over Jobu's Rum Collection on overall offensive depth.
8
Jobu's Rum Collection
BRank #8 — Best Closer Trio in the League
Strengths
Paul Skenes is the reigning NL Cy Young winner — one of the highest weekly SP point scorers in this draft. The best closer scoring trio in the league: Munoz, Pagan, and Hoffman generate save and high-leverage points that most rosters can't match from the bullpen. Contreras is a top-5 weekly scoring catcher.
Concerns
The offense is the thinnest in the top half of this league for point production. After Contreras, the weekly scoring drops off sharply. Willy Adames, Alonso, and Altuve are solid but aging, and the lineup doesn't generate offensive points at a rate that can carry a bad pitching week.
Wild Card
Bryce Miller (on IL). If he returns healthy and pitches at his 2025 level, Jobu's Rum Collection has five deep SP scoring options. Without him, the rotation behind Skenes is thin and points will fluctuate week to week.
Verdict
A pitching-dominant team that will outscore opponents on the mound and fight to stay in matchups offensively. Ranked #8 over Strider? I Barely Know Her purely on roster health — no IL issues to start the season.
9
Strider? I Barely Know Her
B-Rank #9 — Three IL Arms to Open the Season
Strengths
Caminero is a top-3B weekly point scorer in baseball. Freeman is a perennial high-output first baseman. Imanaga and Gilbert are a strong SP 1-2 point punch. Jhoan Duran is an elite high-leverage closer producing save and strikeout points every week he pitches.
Concerns
Three pitchers opening on the IL is a real weekly points problem early on. Rodon (bone spur, late April/May), Musgrove (TJ, no firm timeline), and Bieber (forearm fatigue) are all unavailable at the start. Expect to lose pitching-point matchups in April.
Wild Card
Rodon's May return. Add him to Imanaga/Gilbert/Woo already in place and this suddenly becomes one of the better weekly pitching point totals in the league — a complete team from May onward.
Verdict
Guaranteed slow start — count on going below .500 in April matchups. The mid-season surge potential is real if Rodon returns clean. Ranked #9 over The Crochman Cometh on a more reliable closer scoring situation.
10
The Crochman Cometh
B-Rank #10 — Deep Bench, Volatile Active Roster
Strengths
Yamamoto is an elite weekly SP point anchor. The bench SP depth is arguably the best in the entire league — King, Horton, and Sasaki are three arms most teams would start every week. Devers is a consistent top-10 3B point producer. Chisholm gives speed-plus-power scoring upside.
Concerns
The closer scoring is the weakest active bullpen in the league — Jansen in a committee, Palencia murky, Iglesias inconsistent. Snell's shoulder starts on IL with a 6-week floor and chronic history that makes a full scoring season uncertain. Sale and deGrom are high-risk point gambles.
Wild Card
If Snell returns fully healthy and King/Horton/Sasaki all earn starting roles and deliver, the weekly pitching point total jumps significantly. If Snell re-injures early, Yamamoto is carrying too much weight alone. The team name will age well or become a cruel joke.
Verdict
The widest outcome range of any team in the league — anywhere from 2nd to 11th is realistic. Best reserve pitching depth in the league; worst active closer scoring. A team that will live and die by weekly health.
11
I need healing
B-Rank #11 — Wheeler or Bust
Strengths
Judge and Elly De La Cruz are two of the most explosive offensive point scorers in baseball. Trea Turner at full health is an elite SS point producer. Olson and Arozarena add power-and-speed offensive depth. Brandon Webb is a reliable mid-rotation SP scorer every week he starts.
Wild Card
Zack Wheeler. Threw a minor league game March 23rd — potentially the fastest TOS recovery on record. If he returns in April and scores like his 2025 self (2.47 ERA, elite Ks), this rotation becomes a legitimate top-5 weekly SP point total. If he struggles or re-injures, the staff behind Webb is thin.
Concerns
Hader starts on IL with biceps tendinitis. Seymour is an unproven save scorer. The entire closer point production depends on Hader coming back healthy — and every week he's out is save points this team doesn't recoup. The team name may be the most prophetic in this league.
Verdict
The most binary outcome in the draft. Wheeler comes back clean = legitimate top-5 weekly scorer. Wheeler struggles = bottom-3. Ranked #11 over Jazz J1zz'on'em because at least the pitching staff can compete on a good week.
12
Jazz J1zz'on'em
B-Rank #12 — Best Offense, Worst Pitching
Strengths
The best offensive point trio in the draft — Acuna, Tucker, and Chourio is a spectacular top-3 OF scoring combination. Edward Wetherholt and Pete Alonso add power depth. Jazz J1zz'on'em will put up big offensive point totals every week regardless of matchup.
Fatal Flaw
The pitching is going to bleed points until July. Burnes (TJ surgery, July) and Kelly (back injury) both start on IL. The active rotation — Gore, Rogers, Alcantara, Santana — is below-average by H2H points standards. Opponents can count on a pitching-point advantage in basically every matchup through June.
Wild Card
Corbin Burnes' return in July. When healthy, Burnes is an ace-level weekly point scorer. His return in the second half completely changes the weekly math for this roster — and they're built to peak at exactly the right time for a playoff run.
Verdict
The most polarized roster in the league. Dead last in the power rankings — but the most dangerous second-half team if Burnes returns clean. Do not trade Jazz J1zz'on'em's bats away cheaply in June assuming they're out of it.
